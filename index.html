GeoJSON ToolKit

While working on my Senior Design project, I found myself wanting some simple tools for working with GeoJSON data So, I wrote a validator and object counter and put them in a public repository. There are currently Python and JavaScript implementations, and they should be available on PyPI and npm, respectively.

Senior Design Project

In response to a proposal made by LLNL for a system that could fuse data and interact with networked sensors, our team built a mobile application and a web application to interface a custom pluggable sensor and a remote database. Gathered data can be merged with publicly available information (e.g. Twitter) to allow users to compare and relate large amounts of data. More details are available in our poster and even more in our report.

As Fusion/Visualization Lead and Treasurer, I contributed code and graphics while managing our budget (approximately $2500). More specifically, I built OGRe, a library for fetching data from 3rd-party APIs, and Vizit, a visualizer that can render any GeoJSON file.

Portfolio

I would have to say my computer self-education took a top-down approach since I got interested in web development first. Funny enough, MySpace instigated my learning of HTML and CSS. HTML+CSS led me to JavaScript which, in turn, got me interested in PHP. By that time I took on some simple projects for local businesses. Some of those projects are still up and running (see the site-selector in the upper left corner), but, unfortunately, some are not. I keep replicas of most of the websites I have developed to preserve and showcase my work. Below is a list of sites in my portfolio with some description of the relevant history.

Bayfitted

Bayfitted was the first significant site that I ever built. My good friend, Ben, told me that his uncle, Chris, needed a website for his custom art and clothing business in San Francisco. Since Bayfitted did not already depend on a site, I was able to learn as I went for a real cause. Unfortunately, school and other distractions often interfered with my ability to continuously maintain and develop the site. Bayfitted is no longer in operation, but the site remains viewable.

Managing Life Transitions

After some success with Bayfitted, I convinced my grandmother that I could take over adminstration website. We were able to obtain the source code of her site from the original developer, and I managed her web presence from then on. In mid-2012, my grandmother moved from the Bay Area to northern Arizona leaving behind her 16-year practice, but her website is still viewable.

Saroor Indian Cuisine

Ben approached me again with an opportunity. He introduced me to his friend, Prince, whose parents run an Indian restaurant in Walnut Creek, CA. This time, Ben and I were pretty confident we could make some money from the deal, and we did! I agreed to build the site for $300 which I split with Ben as a finder's commission for both Saroor and Bayfitted. Prince and I have not recently been in touch, but I have continued to keep the site up and, until recently, even paid for the registration myself (albeit for .com instead of .net). I intend to let the registration expire, but the site will remain viewable.

Austin Tailor

My brother, who was trying to start a business at the time, approached me for help with a website. His requirements were more than I had achieved before, but within my reach. Balancing the beginning of my enrollment at UCSC with the development of this more advanced site challenged me in all the right ways. Unfortunately, the business never took off, but the site remains (although it is not viewable because it is PHP-based, which GitHub Pages does not support).
