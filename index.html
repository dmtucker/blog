Configuration Automation

Lately, I've been working on automating the setup of my preferred working environment. It is a rather simple project, but, then again, that is usually a good thing. Anyways, the only novel part (so far) is the customization script that allows you to customize configuration files when they are installed. This is useful for configs that require a password (e.g. irssi).

GeoJSON ToolKit

While working on my Senior Design project, I found myself wanting some simple tools for working with GeoJSON data So, I wrote a validator and object counter and put them in a public repository. There are currently Python and JavaScript implementations, and they are available on PyPI and npm, respectively.

Senior Design Project

In response to a proposal made by LLNL for a system that could fuse data and interact with networked sensors, our team built a mobile application and a web application to interface a custom pluggable sensor and a remote database. Gathered data can be merged with publicly available information (e.g. Twitter) to allow users to compare and relate large amounts of data.

As Fusion/Visualization Lead and Treasurer, I contributed code and graphics while managing our budget (approximately $2500). More specifically, I built OGRe, a library for fetching data from 3rd-party APIs, and Vizit, a visualizer that can render any GeoJSON file.

Sentinel

While trying to devise a better way of managing passwords for many digital accounts, I came up with a memorable scheme that I implemented in an encrypted spreadsheet file. Thinking it would help more people if it were a standalone application, I created a web2py project for a class at UCSC.

Update: I will not be contributing to this project any further. Turns out, I just wanted Keepass all along...

Portfolio

I would have to say my computer self-education took a top-down approach since I got into web development first. Funny enough, MySpace got me intersted in web technologies. HTML+CSS led me to JavaScript which eventually led to PHP, by which time I took on some simple projects for local businesses. Some of those projects are still up and running (see the site-selector in the upper left corner), but, unfortunately, some are not. I keep replicas of all the websites I have developed to perserve and showcase my work. Below is a list of sites in my portfolio with some description of the relevant history.

Bayfitted

Bayfitted was the first significant site that I ever built. My best friend, Ben, told me one day that his uncle, Chris, needed a website for his custom art and clothing business in San Francisco. Since Bayfitted did not already depend on a site, I could learn as I went for a real cause. Unfortunately, school and other distractions have often interfered with my ability to continuously maintain and develop the site. I intend to return to this site very soon and simplify it to appear a more put-together appearance.

Managing Life Transitions

After my respectable success with Bayfitted, my grandmother and I decided the website she had for her Hypnotherapy practice was not worth the price she was paying. We were able to obtain the source code of her site, and I managed her web presence from then on. In mid-2012, my grandmother moved from the Bay Area to northern Arizona leaving behind her 16-year practice, but her website is still viewable.

Saroor Indian Cuisine

With 2 sites under belt, I was feeling pretty confident when Ben approached me again with an opportunity. He introduced me to his friend, Prince, whose parents run an Indian restaurant in Walnut Creek, CA. This time, Ben and I were pretty confident we could make some money from the deal, and we did! I agreed to build the site for $300 which I split with Ben as a finder's commission for both Saroor and Bayfitted. Prince and I have not recently been in touch, but I have continued to keep the site up despite a migration from .net to .com.

Austin Tailor

My brother, who was trying to start a business at the time, approached me for help with a website. His requirements were more than I had achieved before, but were, what I believed to be, within my reach. Balancing the beginning of my enrollment at UCSC with the development of this more advanced site challenged me in all the right ways. Unfortunately, the business never took off, but the site remains.

Santa Cruz Sound

Freshman year of college, we got rowdy. That tends to be difficult without great speakers. I found Santa Cruz Sound online and correctly thought a barter was in order. For the website, we got hooked up big time... literally. I continue to maintain the site.
