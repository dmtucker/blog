Transdisciplinarity of Listening

Here is a short guide that explains how cancer affects the body. It is based on an interview with Hannah Ison.

Humans have 23 chromosomes in the nuclei of their cells.
Chromsomes are made up of DNA.
Different sections of DNA (genes) are expressed as proteins by enzymes.
DNA and RNA Polymerase transcript and translate DNA and RNA, respectively, to make proteins.
The Myc gene plays an important role in initiating the duplication of a cell.
Many cancers rely on the over-expression of the Myc gene.
The Myc gene gets excessively expressed when a virus injects its RNA into a cell.
